My Drawing

My childhood friends cat, Leo, died going on two years ago now. This cat was her LIFE.
Usually we make each other things for birthdays, and I can't knit or crochet like she can so I tend to draw or paint something for her. I decided to recreate a picture I took of Leo through digital art. That picture is what I have chosen to display here on this website.
It made her cry all over her cake, and though that was not the desired outcome (seeing that it had been over a year since he died), I'm glad it was good enough to make her think of the cat instead of my subpar art skills.
It is one of my favourite pieces I've ever done.

Baptism

The end is nothing we could even imagine.
Contemplation is at its peak,
Yet we strive to reach beyond

We are but stardust looking to be planets,
Searching for something, somewhere to take the jagged edges of our humanity
And shape us into a version like itself.

Lies, war and hatred between the children of space
The prisoners of time that will never stop running out.
We kill and destroy and die for a story

A tale told by mouths in tongues unknown to us
Incomprehensible, yet we bow down to a king made of smoke.
A legend of mirrors,
With nothing but darkness at the end of the tunnel, we fall

So take what is mine and sheath me in the moulds
That you did not make nor find
Just trust in, blindly

I am but space dust drifting in infinity
I shape my own jagged edges,
The energy of the universe carries me along to a place I don't yet know

But you keep on waiting,
Scorning the drifters of time and space like we are different from you.
Like you are more than just dust

Lost in the sheath of smoke and mirrors.
Asceticism,
For a king of dust.
